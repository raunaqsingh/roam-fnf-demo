First principles

Three rules we built Roam around.

Two come out of how title and escrow actually work. One comes out of how production AI holds up under real scale.
01
Act inside the workflow
A title file is 30 to 40 discrete steps across AIM+, SoftPro, ResWare, and outbound comms. An AI that only suggests, and asks a human to execute, buys minutes per step. An AI that completes the step inside the system of record compounds across the whole file.
Assist wins minutes. Act wins files.
02
Rules as data, not prompts
1,300 offices, 50 states. California is community property. Texas has its own disclosures. Florida charges doc stamp taxes. Carrier rules vary by underwriter. One monolithic prompt gets edge cases wrong and cannot be audited. Rules expressed as data stay accurate, editable, and versioned.
No single prompt runs 1,300 offices.
03
Humans own the consequential calls
ALTA Best Practices define chain of custody on fund movement, disbursement, and underwriting authority. The agent prepares, routes, logs. Humans own every decision that can cost a claim, a policy, or a license. Approval thresholds and audit trails make the system insurable, not slower.
Automate preparation. Humans own decisions.
These are not opinions borrowed from other industries. They are what the title workflow and the AI stack require.
